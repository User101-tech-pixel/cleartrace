DIY: Remove Yourself from a People‑Search Site (Step‑by‑Step)

By QuietShield Team · Updated 2025-08-26

People‑search sites aggregate your personal details—addresses, phone numbers, relatives—and make them easy to find. Here’s a general, free process you can use to request removal.

1) Find your listing

  • Search your name + city/state on the site (or on Google with site:example.com "Your Name").
  • Copy the profile URL for your specific record.

2) Locate the opt‑out page

  • Scroll to the footer for “Opt‑Out,” “Do Not Sell My Info,” or “Privacy.”
  • Some sites require email verification; others use CAPTCHA or snail‑mail.

3) Submit your request

  • Provide the minimum data necessary to match your record.
  • Use a dedicated email alias for privacy, if possible.

4) Follow up

  • Track confirmation emails and check the live page after a few days.
  • If the listing reappears, resubmit or contact support—persistence matters.
